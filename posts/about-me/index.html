About Me 👻

Posted on Jun 18, 2025

Welcome to my corner of the internet, where things occasionally break, tech tantrums are real, but the passion never dies. I’m that guy who once thought Netlify Identity was a good idea — and lived to tell the tale 😅.

🛠 What I Actually Do

I tinker. I build. I break (sometimes accidentally), then rebuild. My journey started with a blog, turned into battling CMS setups, then dancing with GitHub auth, OAuth app secrets, and deciphering YAML files like ancient treasure maps.

I’m currently exploring the world of open-source, static site generators (Hugo 💖), and building things the way I want — clean, minimal, and without 15 popups yelling at me.

Whether it’s:

  • Spinning up Markdown-based blog sites,
  • Manually pushing images into the static/uploads/ folder from VS Code like a proud craftsman,
  • Or finally ditching bloated CMS platforms for peace of mind…

I’m here for it — one commit at a time.

😤 What I Tried (and survived)

  • Tried Netlify Identity: got ghosted by password reset links.
  • Used Git Gateway: more like Git Gatekeep.
  • Discovered Decap CMS: said “Hello,” then quietly backed away.
  • Finally decided: I’ll write my damn blogs in VS Code, thank you very much.

🤓 What I’m Learning

Still learning how to:

  • Not rage at .env variables.
  • Name image folders better than “img1_final_FINAL.png”.
  • Accept that being a developer sometimes means being your own worst tech support.

But hey, at least my Hugo build doesn’t scream at me anymore.

💭 Why This Blog?

To document the chaos. To share small wins. To maybe make one developer out there feel a little less alone when their CMS collapses mid-deployment.

This blog isn’t about perfection. It’s about learning, growing, and having fun — even if it means spending two hours fixing a front-matter typo. (YAML is a cruel friend.)

🧠 Tech Stack I Love

  • Hugo – Fast, clean, and doesn’t judge you for your folder names.
  • Markdown – My therapist.
  • GitHub – My cloud-based journal.
  • VS Code – Where most of my dreams (and mistakes) happen.

👋 Final Words

If you’ve read this far, you deserve a cookie. 🍪
I’m not here to become the next tech guru — just documenting my journey, one blog post and one mistake at a time.

Let’s keep it raw, real, and as close to the terminal as possible.
No BS. Just bytes.

– Anson
